Idiom of the Week

Up in the Air

This expression is used if you say that a decision or a situation has not yet been completely settled, set, planned, or confirmed.

Similar words:

unresolved uncertain undecided unfinished unsettled pending
Up in the Air — Examples

When to use it

1
The proposal to work with the new client is up in the air as there are various issues regarding budgeting.
2
The meeting date with the client is up in the air.
3
My promotion to be senior manager this year is up in the air.
4
My weekend plans are up in the air.
5
My vacation plans are up in the air because my manager did not approve my dates.
Sentence Practice

Using "Up in the Air" in context

Before
"I have to make some project. The project to work with my cowork, but this project up in the air."
After
"I have to work on a new project with my coworker, but it is up in the air."
make a project → work on a project
cowork → coworker
this project up in the air → it is up in the air
Context

Why is it
"up in the air"?

The boss has not decided whether to do the project or not.
We are short-staffed and may not meet the client's deadline.

Short-staffed:

When a team or company doesn't have enough employees to handle the workload.

Vocabulary

Tech-Savvy

Savvy refers to having practical knowledge, shrewdness, and good judgment in specific areas. It describes someone who is well-informed, perceptive, and quick-witted — often used to describe someone "in the know".
"I am a programmer, so I am very tech-savvy."
"I am not a programmer, but I am tech-savvy."
tech-savvy media-savvy business-savvy
Vocabulary

Fun & Coding

Different ways to say something is enjoyable:
fun interesting entertaining enjoyable
"It is very fun and interesting to do coding with Claude Code."

What do you call someone who codes?

Coder (casual) Developer (professional) Programmer (general)
Useful Phrases

Tell me vs.
Remind me

Tell me / Inform me

Share new information I don't know yet.

"I want you to tell me what I need to do."

Remind me

Help me remember something I already know but forgot.

"I want you to remind me what I need to do."

Week 3 Recap

✓ Up in the Air — undecided, not yet confirmed
✓ Short-staffed — not enough people
✓ Tech-savvy — knowledgeable about technology
✓ Tell me (new info) vs. Remind me (forgotten info)
✓ work on a project (not "make" a project)
